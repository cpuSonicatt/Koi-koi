Koi-koi

Terms

  • Hanafuda - the name of the deck of cards.
  • the Oya - “parent”; dealer.
  • the Ko - “child”; the other player.
  • Field - the face-up cards on the table.
  • Point pile - where you put your matched cards on the table.
  • a yaku - a special combination of cards that score points.
  • “Koi-koi” - “Come on!”; the signifier to continue playing the round.
  • “Stop” - the signifier to stop playing the round.
  • Month(s) - the suits that make up the deck.

Objective

Make the best combination of cards by matching based on the month.

Deal

Choose an Oya (either by random or by both players drawing a single card and whoever’s card is earliest. If both are the same month, whichever is highest in value. If both are the same value, retry.)

Deal 8 cards to Ko face down, then 8 cards to field face up, then 8 cards face down to yourself. Set aside the rest of the cards to form a draw pile.

Play

The game is typically played across 12 rounds, corresponding to each month of the year (round 1 is January, round 2 is February, etc.).

Starting with the Oya, match a card by month/plant from your hand with one on the field, and move both to your point pile. If you can’t make a match, choose a card from your hand and place it on the field.

Then, draw 1 card from the top of the draw pile, and if it matches with a card on the field, move both to your point pile. If you can’t make a match, place the card on the field.

If a player is able to match 2 cards on the field with one in their hand or from the draw pile, they must make a choice. If a player is able to match 3 cards on the field with one in their hand or from the draw pile, they collect all cards from that suit and take them to their point pile.

If the current player has scored a yaku, they must choose if they wish to “Koi-koi”, or “Stop”.

  • If the current player calls “Koi-koi”, then play continues as normal until another yaku is made. The opposing player’s possible score is multiplied with each call.
    • If the player who previously called “Koi-koi” gets another yaku, they can then decide whether they wish to “Koi-koi”, or “Stop”.
    • If the player who didn’t previously call “Koi-koi” gets a yaku, play stops and that player gets their yaku score, multiplied by however many times the other played called “Koi-koi”, plus 1. (i.e. if Player A called “Koi-koi” once, the multiplier for the other player would be x2. Twice would be x3. Thrice would be x4, etc.)
  • If the current player called “Stop”, then the round is over and points are tallied up. The current player then becomes the Oya.

If both players end up with empty hands, with or without a yaku, and no player has called “Stop”, then the round is at a stalemate, and no points are awarded.

Yaku/Scoring

Yaku can stack to score more points. If you score 7 or more points, then your score is doubled.

FIVE LIGHTS - 10 points - All light cards

DRY FOUR LIGHTS - 8 points - All light cards excluding Rain

RAINY FOUR LIGHTS - 7 points - 4 light cards including Rain

Any combination of 4 light cards and the Rain card.

THREE LIGHTS - 6 points - 3 light cards excluding Rain

Any combination of 3 light cards without the Rain card.

BOAR-DEER-BUTTERFLY - 5 points

ANIMALS - 1 point (+ 1 for each additional Animal card) - 5 or more Animal cards

RED POETRY - 5 points

BLUE POETRY - 5 points

SLIPS - 1 point (+ 1 for each additional Paper card) - 5 or more Slip cards

MONTHLY - 4 points - 4 cards of the same month in the corresponding round

If you don’t plan to play 12 rounds, you can ignore the corresponding round condition.

MOON VIEWING - 6 points

Optional.

CHERRY BLOSSOM VIEWING - 6 points

Optional.

CHAFF - 1 point (+ 1 for each additional Chaff card) - 10 or more Chaff cards

The Sake Cup card counts as Chaff as well as Animal.

Instant wins and re-deals

If either player has been dealt:

  • a 4 pairs of cards (i.e. any 2 from any 4 months);
  • all 4 cards of the same month

then they get 6 points, and a re-deal occurs.

If either of these hands are dealt to the table, then the round is declared void and a re-deal occurs.
